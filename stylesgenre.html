Styles Genre

Styles in art are distinct approaches or techniques that define how an artwork is created, such as Impressionism, which focuses on light and color, or Cubism, which uses geometric shapes and multiple perspectives. They help categorize art based on visual elements and artistic methods.

Starry Night

"Starry Night" is a masterpiece by Vincent van Gogh, painted in 1889 during his stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France. The swirling skies and vibrant colors reflect Van Gogh's emotional intensity and fascination with the night. Inspired by his observations and imagination, the painting showcases his deep connection to nature and the cosmos. Despite his struggles with mental health, Van Gogh used art as a means of expression, blending reality with his unique vision. Tragically, he gained little recognition during his lifetime, but today, his work is celebrated worldwide.

Artist: Vincent van Gogh

Estimated Value: $1.1 million

The Persistence Of Memory

The author of The Persistence of Memory is Salvador Dalí. This surrealist masterpiece, created in 1931, features melting clocks as a reflection of the fluidity of time and the irrational nature of dreams. Dalí drew inspiration from Freud's theories of the subconscious and his own Catalonian landscapes. The painting emerged during the height of the Surrealist movement and is emblematic of Dalí's fascination with memory, decay, and the intersection of reality and imagination.

Artist: Salvador Dalí

Estimated Value: $23.99

The Scream

The Scream is a masterpiece by Norwegian artist Edvard Munch. The painting reflects Munch's deep emotional turmoil and existential anxiety, inspired by a personal experience of feeling overwhelmed while walking at sunset, where the sky turned blood red, evoking a "scream" in nature. Created in 1893, it embodies the angst of modern humanity and the psychological intensity of Symbolism. Munch, a pioneer of Expressionism, used vivid colors and distorted forms to convey raw emotion, influenced by his troubled childhood and fascination with death and despair. The work’s impact remains profound, symbolizing universal human fear and introspection.

Artist: Edvard Munch

Estimated Value: $119.9 million

Red Canna

Red Canna is a painting by Georgia O’Keeffe, a renowned American modernist artist. Inspired by the intricate beauty of flowers, O’Keeffe magnified their forms to reveal their sensual and abstract qualities. Through "Red Canna," she explored nature’s vibrant colors and soft curves, inviting viewers to see flowers from a new perspective. Painted in 1924, this work reflects her fascination with organic forms and her deep connection to the natural world. O’Keeffe's groundbreaking approach positioned her as a leading figure in modern art, celebrated for her bold, evocative imagery.

Artist: Georgia O’Keeffe

Estimated Value: $343

Woman With a Hat

"Woman with a Hat" is a renowned painting by Henri Matisse, created in 1905. This artwork marked a pivotal moment in the Fauvist movement, showcasing Matisse's bold use of non-naturalistic colors to convey emotion rather than realism. Inspired by his wife Amélie, the subject of the painting, Matisse experimented with vibrant hues and dynamic brushwork to break from traditional artistic conventions. The piece sparked controversy at the 1905 Salon d’Automne in Paris but solidified Matisse as a leader of Fauvism. Born in 1869 in France, Matisse is celebrated for revolutionizing modern art with his innovative exploration of color and form.

Artist: Henri Matisse

Estimated Value: $285

Ophelia

Ophelia is a painting by John Everett Millais, a prominent member of the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood. The artwork, created between 1851 and 1852, depicts the tragic drowning of Ophelia from Shakespeare's Hamlet. Millais meticulously painted the natural setting along the Hogsmill River in Surrey, reflecting the Pre-Raphaelites’ commitment to detail and truth to nature. The model for Ophelia was Elizabeth Siddal, who posed in a bathtub filled with water to capture the lifelike expression of despair and fragility. The painting is celebrated for its vibrant colors, symbolic flora, and emotional depth, embodying Millais' inspiration from both literary and natural beauty.

Artist: John Everett Millais

Estimated Value: $598

Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I

"Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I" was painted by Gustav Klimt in 1907. Klimt, a prominent Austrian symbolist painter, was inspired by the opulence and decadence of the time, blending symbolic and decorative styles. The artwork was commissioned by Ferdinand Bloch-Bauer, a wealthy industrialist, to depict his wife, Adele. Klimt's use of gold leaf and intricate patterns reflects his fascination with the mystical and the sensual. This piece became a symbol of the fusion between art and the luxurious world of Viennese high society. It gained significant attention and historical value, especially after being seized by Nazis and later repatriated to the Bloch-Bauer family.

Artist: Gustav Klimt

Estimated Value: $135 million

Composition VIII

The author of Composition VIII is Wassily Kandinsky, a pioneer of abstract art. Created in 1923, this work reflects Kandinsky's belief in the spiritual power of art, using geometric shapes, vibrant colors, and dynamic forms to evoke harmony and emotion. Inspired by music's ability to convey meaning without words, Kandinsky sought to translate this into visual art. The painting demonstrates his transition to more structured compositions, influenced by his Bauhaus tenure. Kandinsky, a Russian-born artist, was instrumental in shaping modern art through his theoretical writings and innovative abstractions.

Artist: Wassily Kandinsky

Estimated Value: $175.75
