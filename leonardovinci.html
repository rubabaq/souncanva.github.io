Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519)

Leonardo da Vinci was a polymath, artist, inventor, and scientist, often regarded as one of the greatest figures in history. Born in Vinci, Italy, his works embody the Renaissance ideal. He is best known for his masterpieces such as Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, which demonstrate his skill in capturing human emotion and his deep understanding of anatomy and perspective. Leonardo was also a visionary inventor, producing designs for many devices far ahead of his time.

Leonardo's Work

Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci around 1503-1506, reflects his mastery of capturing human emotion and realism. Inspired by the serene beauty of Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a Florentine merchant, da Vinci focused on creating a sense of mystery with her subtle smile and gaze. His use of sfumato—a technique of blending colors and tones—brought depth and lifelike quality to her face. Da Vinci’s fascination with human anatomy and nature is evident in the piece’s meticulous detail. The Mona Lisa became a symbol of the Renaissance’s blend of art, science, and observation.
