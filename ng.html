National Gallery (London, UK)


The National Gallery in London, located in Trafalgar Square, London is one of the most renowned art museums in the world. It was established in 1824 when the British government purchased a collection of 38 paintings from banker John Julius Angerstein to make art accessible to the public. Over time, the collection grew through acquisitions, donations, and bequests, representing some of the greatest masterpieces in Western European art from the 13th to the 19th centuries. The building itself, designed by William Wilkins, opened in 1838 and has since undergone several expansions to accommodate its growing collection. Today, the National Gallery houses iconic works by artists such as Leonardo da Vinci, Vincent van Gogh, Claude Monet, and Rembrandt, attracting millions of visitors annually. It remains a cornerstone of cultural life in the UK, offering free admission to its permanent collection.
