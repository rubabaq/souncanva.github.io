Vincent van Gogh (1853–1890)

Vincent van Gogh was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter whose bold, expressive works have had a profound influence on 20th-century art. Van Gogh struggled with mental illness throughout his life, which greatly impacted his art. His famous works include Starry Night, Sunflowers, and The Bedroom. Although he only gained fame posthumously, his emotionally charged paintings and innovative techniques earned him a central place in the history of art.

Vincent’s Work

Starry Night

Starry Night by Vincent van Gogh, created in 1889, is one of his most iconic paintings. It was inspired by his time in the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, where he was grappling with mental health struggles. The swirling sky, with its vivid blues and yellows, symbolizes van Gogh's emotional turbulence and longing for peace. The painting captures a dreamlike quality, blending reality and imagination. Despite the chaos in his mind, the bright stars represent hope. Van Gogh's use of bold, expressive brushstrokes conveys his internal struggle and search for meaning in a turbulent world.

Sunflowers

Vincent van Gogh’s Sunflowers (1888) was a series of still lifes that celebrated the vibrant beauty of sunflowers. Inspired by his move to Arles, France, van Gogh sought to capture the warmth and joy of nature. The series was meant to adorn the room of his friend, artist Paul Gauguin, who was visiting. Through bold, swirling brushstrokes and vivid yellow tones, van Gogh conveyed both the beauty and transience of life. The sunflowers, while radiant, also hint at decay, a reflection of van Gogh’s own struggles with mental health and his search for meaning. This iconic work is now celebrated for its emotional depth and innovation in color and texture.

Estimated Value: $450
The Bedroom

The Bedroom by Vincent van Gogh, painted in 1888, captures a deeply personal moment of calm and isolation. Van Gogh created the painting while staying in Arles, France, and it reflects his desire for tranquility and stability. The simple composition, featuring his unadorned bed and room, symbolizes his longing for peace after struggles with his mental health. The vibrant colors and distorted perspective evoke emotional intensity, demonstrating Van Gogh's innovative use of color and form to express inner experiences. It was part of his broader quest to find solace in everyday life amid his turbulent emotional state.

Estimated Value: $225
Irises

"Irises" by Vincent van Gogh, painted in 1889, captures the artist’s emotional state and connection to nature during his time at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence. Van Gogh, struggling with mental health, found solace in painting the vibrant irises outside his room. This work, a symbol of his desire to reconnect with life and creativity, is filled with expressive brushwork and bold color. Van Gogh’s technique emphasizes the energy and texture of the flowers, and it was one of the first pieces that brought him significant recognition, later becoming part of the collection of the J. Paul Getty Museum

Wheatfield With Crows

Wheatfield with Crows by Vincent van Gogh, painted in July 1890, is often interpreted as a reflection of the artist's emotional turmoil. The dramatic sky and swirling crows suggest a sense of foreboding and unrest. Van Gogh, in a state of mental distress, was drawn to the countryside, seeking solace in nature. The field of wheat symbolizes both life and death, a duality he frequently explored. It’s believed that this piece was created shortly before his tragic death, encapsulating his inner struggle and the feeling of isolation that permeated his final days.

Estimated Value: $384
